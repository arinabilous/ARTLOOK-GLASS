GLASS FLOOR

LAMINATED GLASS FLOORS.

The Laminated glass flooring is produced by bonding two or more layers of heat-treated pressed glass together with a plastic resin interlayer, which is known as Polyvinyl Butyral (PVB). Multiple PVB glass sheets are more popularly used by the automobile industry and the airline industry for designing windshields. The The PVB interlayer makes glass floors impervious to water damage, it provides sound reduction and blocks 99% UV rays. PVB interlayers can be customized into different colored sheets for use by glass manufacturers for architects and construction engineers.

The best way to lay a laminated glass floor is by using block pavers with an aluminum grid. This newer method encases the sides and the bottom edges of each glass opens the block with a silicone sealant rather than the original method of using the grout. The aluminum grid provides durability and reinforcement for foot traffic, as well as offering a unique interior design element. Glass flooring is available in several different options that we offer in New York Tempering Glass including crystal clear which is transparent, frost, revo, small rocks and sand. These options provide a different level of translucency and privacy, while natural lighting can still filter in.

Glass floor technology is safe, safe, quake proof, can handle all day foot traffic and shatterproof. Iconic structures like The Eiffel Tower are being renovated to place glass floors on its first floor level to give tourists a striking view of the street below. Government buildings, educational institutions and health care facilities all enjoy the environmentally friendly material, its aesthetics and its sanitary ability to repel powders, molds, bacteria and requires less cleaning than other floor materials. New York Tempering Glass manufactures and installs all types and styles of glass flooring.
